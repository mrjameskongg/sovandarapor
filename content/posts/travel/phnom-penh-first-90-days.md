# Phnom Penh: First 90 Days

## The Basics

**Rent:** $400/month for a decent 1BR in BKK1  
**Food:** $3-8/day eating local  
**Visa:** Business visa on arrival, extend monthly  

## Reality Check

The infrastructure is improving rapidly, but patience is required. Power cuts happen. Internet can be spotty. Banking as a foreigner takes time.

But the energy here is infectious. Everyone is building something. The government is surprisingly tech-friendly. And at these costs, you can focus on creating rather than just surviving.

## Key Lessons

1. Build relationships first. Business follows.
2. Learn basic Khmer phrases - it opens doors.
3. Always carry cash. Card payments are hit-or-miss.
4. Rainy season (May-October) is serious. Plan accordingly.

The city has its rough edges, but there's something special happening here.
