# The Illusion of the Separate Self

What we call "I" is just a collection of thoughts, sensations, and perceptions arising in awareness. There is no separate entity having these experiences.

## The Investigation

Through careful investigation, we can see that the self is more like a process than a thing - a continuous flow of experiences without a central experiencer.

When you look for the "I" that claims to be having thoughts, where do you find it? The thought "I am thinking" arises in awareness, but where is the thinker of this thought?

## Direct Seeing

The mind creates the story of a separate self through memory and anticipation. But in this moment, what is actually here? Just pure awareness, appearing as whatever is present.

This isn't a philosophical position but a direct seeing. The separate self is not eliminated - it's simply recognized as a misunderstanding of what we already are.
